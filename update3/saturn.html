Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is best known for its stunning and expansive ring system, which is the most prominent in the solar system. A gas giant, Saturn is composed mostly of hydrogen and helium and has a diameter about 9 times that of Earth. Despite its massive size, Saturn has a very low densityâ€”so low that it could float in water if there were a body of water large enough. The planet's atmosphere is primarily made up of hydrogen and helium, with clouds of ammonia and methane, and is marked by distinct bands of clouds, similar to those of Jupiter, but with less contrast in color. Saturn's rings are made of countless small particles of ice and rock, and while they are relatively thin, they stretch up to 280,000 kilometers (175,000 miles) from the planet's surface. Saturn has at least 82 moons, with Titan being the largest. Titan is of particular interest because it has a thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane, making it a key target for future exploration in the search for prebiotic conditions. Saturn is also known for its strong magnetic field and the presence of a faint, diffuse ring system composed of icy particles. Its iconic appearance and the mysteries of its rings and moons make Saturn a central focus in planetary science and space exploration.
